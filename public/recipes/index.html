Wiener Kaffeekoch

Traditional Viennese Coffee Cake with coffee-soaked rolls and whipped cream

medium 30 minutes
coffee desserts
View Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole Day-old rolls
  • 375 ml Strong coffee
  • 100 g Butter
  • 125 g Sugar
  • 1 packet Vanilla sugar
  • 50 g Chopped almonds
  • 3 whole Eggs
  • 250 ml Cream
  • 1 tsp Instant coffee powder

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rolls in coffee
  2. Beat butter until fluffy, mix well with sugar, vanilla sugar and egg yolks
  3. Add chopped almonds
  4. Squeeze out rolls well and mix with the butter mixture
  5. Fold in stiffly beaten egg whites
  6. Fill into greased ring form
  7. Bake in preheated oven for about 45 minutes at 200-220°C
  8. Turn cake onto a plate and fill the middle with coffee-whipped cream

Brennans Brûlot

A flambéed coffee drink with cognac and citrus

medium 15 minutes
coffee beverages
View Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole Cinnamon stick
  • 12 whole Cloves
  • 2 whole Unwaxed lemons (peeled in strips)
  • 2 whole Unwaxed oranges (peeled in strips)
  • 0.5 whole Orange (juiced)
  • 6 whole Sugar cubes
  • 250 ml Cognac
  • 2 glasses Curaçao
  • 1 L Strong coffee

Instructions:

  1. In a flambé pan, crush cinnamon stick, cloves, citrus peels, and sugar cubes
  2. Pour over cognac and Curaçao, stir and flambé
  3. Keep stirring until sugar dissolves
  4. Add orange juice and deglaze with hot coffee
  5. Fill into preheated cups and serve

Mocca-Menthe

Coffee with dark chocolate and peppermint liqueur

medium 20 minutes
coffee beverages
View Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 100 g Dark chocolate
  • 4 cups Hot strong coffee
  • 8 tbsp Sugar
  • 4 small glasses Peppermint liqueur
  • 250 ml Cream

Instructions:

  1. Slowly melt chocolate in a pot with 3 tbsp water
  2. Add hot coffee and sugar, stir well
  3. Remove pot from heat and add peppermint liqueur
  4. Beat in 3/4 of the cream with a whisk
  5. Distribute into 4 cups
  6. Whip remaining cream until stiff and place on top

Teeauflauf

Tea soufflé with milk and eggs

medium 30 minutes
tea desserts
View Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 L Milk
  • 3 tbsp Tea
  • 125 g Sugar
  • 100 g Butter
  • 100 g Cornstarch
  • 1 pinch Salt
  • 4 whole Eggs

Instructions:

  1. Bring milk with sugar to boil, pour over tea and let steep for 5 minutes
  2. Strain and let cool
  3. Make a light roux with butter and cornstarch
  4. Deglaze with tea milk and cook into thick sauce
  5. Stir in egg yolks and salt
  6. Beat egg whites until stiff and fold into slightly cooled mixture
  7. Fill into greased soufflé dish
  8. Bake in preheated oven for about 40 minutes

Notes:

Serve with compote or rum-soaked fruits

Altenglische Teebowle

Old English tea bowl with port wine and cucumber

medium 15 minutes
tea beverages
View Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole Cucumber
  • 2 bottles Port wine
  • 2 small glasses Brandy
  • 1 L Tea (normal strength)
  • 1 handful Fresh mint leaves

Instructions:

  1. Peel cucumber, halve, remove seeds carefully and dice the flesh
  2. Put cucumber dice in a punch bowl and pour over 1 bottle port wine and brandy
  3. Cover and let rest in refrigerator for 1 hour
  4. Pour over ice-cold tea and second bottle of port wine
  5. Strain into glasses and garnish each with mint leaves

Notes:

Serve ice cold
